Alberto Santos Dumont

The father of aviation

The life of Santos Dumont

  • 1873 - Born in Santos Dumont, Minas Gerais, Brasil. Formely Palmira.
  • 1888 - At the age of 15, saw his first manned flight. Stanley Spencer flew in a baloon and landed with a parachute.
  • 1891 - Started to develop an interest about the mechanical world. Especially, about the internal combustion engine. Resulting in the creaton of a engineless baloon.
  • 1903 - Recieved a grant from the town hall of Ribeirão Preto to continue his research.
  • 1898 to 1905 - Built a lot of models, from n-1 to n-10, they were airships. from n-11 to n-13, started to build model airplanes and a helicopter. The n-14 had two versions (n-14a and n-14b), it was a airship, it had two versions because of the engine used in each version.
  • 1906 - Finally built the model airplane 14-bis. A plane attached to a hydrogen balloon to reduce weight and facilitate takeoff.
  • 1907 to 1908 - He didn't stop to build aeroplanes, after 14-bis came the n-15 and n-16 (a mix of airship and plane). He even built two aquatic slide, n-17 and n-18, used for test the format of the wing submerged.
  • 1909 - Built the Demoiselle Nº20, considered the first ultralight ever made. It was used to train the soldiers of the Great War, due to its low cost and high safety. The engine used in this plane was a original creation of Santos Dumont. This plane consolidated Dumont's role in the birth of aviation in the XX century.
  • 1932 - Died at the age of 59. His name was used to rename the city he was born.
